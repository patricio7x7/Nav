Grammar Patterns — Section 02 · N5 Depth + Start of N4
How to useLook patterns up when you hit them in stories — don't pre-study. Each pattern is one concept with one table. The ~you ni forms and ~tara conditional will appear everywhere in real speech. Focus on those first.
~te ageru / ~te morau / ~te kureru — giving & receiving actions

The most important set of patterns in natural Japanese. Who does the action for whom — and which direction favors whom — changes the word. Native speakers use these constantly; forgetting them makes speech sound cold.

PatternMeaningExample
~te agerudo [verb] for someone elseOshiete ageru yo.I'll show/tell you (doing a favor for you).
~te morauhave someone do [verb] for meOshiete moratta.I had them teach me / they taught me (for my benefit).
~te kurerusomeone does [verb] for me (their initiative)Oshiete kureta.They taught me (on their own, as a favor to me).
~te moraemasu ka?could you do [verb] for me? (polite request)Misete moraemasu ka?Could you show me?
~te kudasaiplease do [verb] (direct polite request)Kite kudasai.Please come.
Key distinctionageru = you → them  ·  morau = them → you (you get the benefit)  ·  kureru = they → you (spontaneous gift of action)
~n desu / ~ndesu — explanation & context

Adds a layer of "and here's why / the deal is..." — softens statements, invites dialogue, shows you're sharing context not just facts. One of the most-used patterns in real conversation.

FormHow to build itExample
verb + n desuplain form + n desuIku n desu.The thing is, I'm going. / I'm going (explaining).
i-adj + n desuplain adj + n desuIsogashii n desu.The thing is, I'm busy.
noun / na-adj + na n desu[noun/na-adj] + na n desuRyokou na n desu.It's a trip, you see.
casual: ~ n daplain form + n daIku n da.I'm going, you see. (casual explanation)
question: ~ n desu ka?adds "and is that so?"Byouki na n desu ka?Oh, are you sick? (seeking explanation)
~ga hoshii — wanting a thing (noun)

For wanting a noun. Different from ~tai which is for wanting to do a verb. Conjugates like an i-adjective.

PatternExampleMeaning
[noun] ga hoshiiAtarashii kaban ga hoshii.I want a new bag.
hoshiku naiSore wa hoshiku nai.I don't want that.
hoshikattaZutto hoshikatta n da.I've wanted it for a long time.
~te hoshiiChanto itte hoshii.I want you to say it properly. (want someone to do something)
~koto ga dekiru — ability (more formal than dekiru alone)

Attach to the dictionary form. More formal/explicit than just using dekiru.

FormExampleMeaning
[dict form] + koto ga dekiruOyogu koto ga dekimasu.I can swim.
negativeOyogu koto ga dekimasen.I can't swim.
casualNihongo ga hanaseru.I can speak Japanese. (potential form — same idea)
~tari ~tari suru — doing things like X and Y

List actions without exhausting every option. Sounds natural when describing routines or typical behavior. Form: past casual (~ta) → swap final a for i, add ri.

How to buildExampleMeaning
[~ta form] → [~tari] × 2 + suruYondari kaitari suru.I do things like read and write.
past: ~tari ~tari shitaSanpo shitari, kaimono shitari shita.I did things like take a walk and go shopping.
with shimauWasure tari suru kara ki wo tsukete.I sometimes forget (lit: do things like forget), so be careful.
~tara — if / when conditional (most common conditional)

Most versatile Japanese conditional. Use it freely in speech. Form: past casual (~ta) + ra. Both "if" and "when" depending on context.

FormExampleMeaning
[~ta] + raAme ga futtara, ie ni iru.If it rains, I'll stay home.
past event: when [did]Uchi ni kaettara, mou nete ita.When I got home, they were already asleep.
with questionShitsumon saretara, dou suru?What will you do if they ask questions?
advice: ~tara iiHayaku neta ra ii yo.You should sleep early. (lit: if you sleep early, it'd be good)
regret: ~tara yokattaHayaku itta ra yokatta.I should have said it earlier.
~ba — if conditional (more formal / hypothetical)

More hypothetical or literary than ~tara. Used for advice, regret, and "if only" feelings. Verb: change final ~u to ~eba. i-adjective: ~i → ~kereba.

FormExampleMeaning
verb: ~u → ~ebaIkeba wakaru yo.If you go, you'll understand.
i-adj: ~i → ~kerebaYasukere ba ii noni.If only it were cheaper.
regret: ~ba yokattaChanto katazukereba yokatta.I should have tidied up properly.
advice: ~ba iiKotaereba ii n dayo.All you have to do is answer.
~you ni suru — making an effort to / trying to (habit/change)

You are actively working to change a behavior or establish a habit. Not a one-time action — an ongoing effort.

FormExampleMeaning
[dict form] + you ni suruHayaku neru you ni shite iru.I'm making an effort to sleep early.
[nai form] + you ni suruOsoku naranai you ni shite iru.I'm trying not to be late.
past: ~you ni shitaAruku you ni shita.I started making an effort to walk.
~you ni naru — coming to / having come to (gradual change)

Something changed over time — an ability developed, a habit formed, a feeling shifted. Not a deliberate effort (that's ~you ni suru) — just how things turned out.

FormExampleMeaning
[dict form] + you ni naruNihongo ga hanaseru you ni natta.I've come to be able to speak Japanese.
[nai form] + you ni naruAmari nomanai you ni natta.I've come to drink less.
gradual shiftSuki ni natte kita.I've started to like it (gradually).
~te oku — doing in advance / leaving as is

Two uses: (1) do something now for future benefit, (2) leave something as is (often intentionally). Both express intentionality about timing.

UseExampleMeaning
do in advanceYoyaku shite okimashita.I booked it in advance (so we're set).
set something upOshiete oite ageru.I'll let you know ahead of time.
leave as isSore wa sono mama ni shite oku.I'll leave that as is (for now).
casual: ~tokuKangaetoku.I'll think about it (casual contraction).
~node — because (soft / explanatory)

Softer than kara. Presents the reason as a given fact, less forceful. Often preferred in polite speech and when explaining circumstances.

FormExampleMeaning
plain form + nodeIsogashii node, ikemasen.Because I'm busy, I can't go. (explaining circumstances)
noun / na-adj + na nodeKaigi na node, ato de.Since it's a meeting, (let's do it) later.
vs karaIsogashii kara, ikemasen.I'm busy so I can't go. (more assertive / causal)
~noni — even though / despite (complaint / contrast)

Signals that the speaker expected something different. Often carries frustration, disappointment, or surprise. Very common in emotional speech.

FormExampleMeaning
plain form + noniIsshokenmei yatte iru noni, umaku ikanai.Even though I'm trying my hardest, it's not going well.
noun / na-adj + na noniIi tenki na noni, ienai yo ne.It's nice weather, yet we can't go out.
at end of sentenceItte kurereba yokatta noni.I wish you had told me... (trailing complaint)
Relative clauses — [verb/adj] + noun

Japanese relative clauses come BEFORE the noun, not after. There is no "that/which/who" — the modifying clause simply sits in front. One of the most important structures to internalize.

StructureExampleMeaning
[verb (plain)] + nounKinou tabeta ramenThe ramen I ate yesterday
[neg verb] + nounMada mite inai eigaA movie I haven't seen yet
[i-adj] + nounTakai kurumaAn expensive car
[na-adj + na] + nounKirei na machiA beautiful town
embedded in sentenceKinou au hito wa dare desu ka?Who is the person you're meeting tomorrow?

Ojisan

[aa] · Midori Shobo · [ne=confirming]. know [shitteru] · [yo=assertion]. here [koko] [kara=from] · straight [massugu] · go [itte] · second [futatsu me] [no=possessive] · traffic light [shingou] · [de=at] · right [migi] [ni=to] · turn [magatte]. do that [sou suru to=when you do] · left side [hidari gawa] [ni=on] · should see [mieru hazu=expected to appear] · [da] · [yo=confident]

Kenta

Futatsu me no shingou de migi desu ne. Wakarimashita. Aruite dono gurai kakarimasu ka?

second [no=possessive] · traffic light · [de=at] · right · [desu] · [ne=confirming what I heard]. understood [wakarimashita]. walking [aruite] · how long [dono gurai] · does it take [kakarimasu] · [ka=question]

Right at the second traffic light. Got it. About how long does it take on foot?

Ojisan

Sou da ne... gofun ka roppun gurai ka na. Sou tooku nai yo.

[sou da ne=hmm let me see, thinking filler]... 5 min · or [ka] · 6 min · about [gurai] · [ka na=I think, casual estimate]. that · far · not [nai] · [yo=reassurance]

Let's see... about five or six minutes I think. It's not that far.

Kenta

Yokatta! Arigatou gozaimasu. Hontou ni tasukarimashita.

great/relief [yokatta=was good]! thank you [arigatou gozaimasu]. truly · was helped [tasukarimashita=received help, passive benefit]

What a relief! Thank you so much. You've really helped me out.

Ojisan

Ii yo, ki wo tsukete ne.

fine [ii] · [yo=warm dismissal]. take care [ki wo tsukete] · [ne=warm closing]

Of course, take care.

narration

Kenta wa ojisan ga oshiete kureta toori ni arukimashita. Shingou wo watatte migi ni magatta totan ni, kireina mise ga me ni hairimashita.

Kenta [wa=topic] · old man [ojisan] [ga=subject] · told [oshiete kureta=told for my benefit] · just as [toori ni] · walked. traffic light [wo=obj] · crossed [watatte] · right [migi] [ni=to] · turned [magatta] · the very moment [totan ni] · nice [kireina] · shop [ga=subject] · eyes [me] [ni=into] · came [hairimashita=entered view]

Kenta
